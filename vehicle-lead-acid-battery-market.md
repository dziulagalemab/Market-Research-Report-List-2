<p><ul><li>Open Battery</li><li>Valve Regulated Battery</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In the vehicle lead acid battery market, there are two types of batteries: open battery and valve regulated battery. </p><p>1. Open Battery: This type of battery allows the free circulation of air within the battery compartment. It requires regular maintenance, such as checking the water level, adding distilled water, and cleaning the terminals. Open batteries are generally less expensive, but they have a higher risk of electrolyte evaporation and can emit potentially harmful gases during charging.</p><p>2. Valve Regulated Battery (VRB): Unlike open batteries, VRBs are sealed and do not require regular maintenance. They utilize a valve system that controls the release of gases, allowing for safe and efficient operation. VRBs are more expensive than open batteries but offer advantages, including longer lifespan, lower self-discharge rates, and the ability to operate in various positions without leakage.</p></p>
