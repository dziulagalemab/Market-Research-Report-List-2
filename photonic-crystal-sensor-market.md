<p><p>A photonic crystal sensor is a type of optical sensor that utilizes photonic crystals to enhance the sensing capabilities. Photonic crystals are periodic structures with a specific arrangement of dielectric materials that can manipulate the propagation of electromagnetic waves, such as light.</p><p>The photonic crystal sensor works by changing the optical properties of the crystal when it interacts with analytes or target substances. This change in the crystal's optical properties, such as refractive index or transmission spectra, is then measured and used to detect and quantify the analyte. Photonic crystal sensors offer numerous advantages, including high sensitivity, label-free detection, and compatibility with different sensing modes (e.g., transmission, reflection, or surface plasmon resonance).</p><p>The photonic crystal sensor market is witnessing significant growth and is expected to continue expanding during the forecast period.
