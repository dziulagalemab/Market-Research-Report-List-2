<p><p>Electron multipliers are devices used for amplifying low levels of electric current. There are different types of electron multipliers present in the market, including Channel Electron Multipliers (CEM) and Continuous-dynode multipliers. CEMs consist of a series of parallel channels that receive and amplify electrons, resulting in a higher output current. Continuous-dynode multipliers have a sequence of closely spaced electrodes called dynodes that multiply the electrons through a cascading process, leading to an augmented current. Both types of multipliers play a significant role in various scientific instruments, such as mass spectrometers and electron microscopes.</p></p>
