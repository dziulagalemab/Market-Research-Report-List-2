<p><p>The raw pecan market consists of two main types: in-shell pecans and shelled pecans. In-shell pecans are those that are still enclosed within their hard outer shell, requiring cracking or shelling before consumption. These are often favored by consumers who enjoy the process of cracking the nuts themselves. Shelled pecans, on the other hand, have already had their shells removed, offering convenience and ease of consumption. These are commonly used in cooking, baking, and as a topping for various dishes, making them popular among chefs and home cooks.</p></p>
