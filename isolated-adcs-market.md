<p><p>Isolated ADCs (Analog-to-digital converters) are electronic devices that convert analog signals into digital data while maintaining electrical isolation between the input and output. There are several market types in isolated ADCs including CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor), LVDS (Low-voltage Differential Signaling), parallel, and others. CMOS ADCs utilize complementary pairs of transistors for efficient data conversion. LVDS ADCs use differential signaling to transmit data over long distances with reduced noise. Parallel ADCs convert multiple analog inputs simultaneously. Other market types may include specialized variations based on specific requirements or technologies, offering unique features or advantages in isolated ADCs applications.</p></p>
