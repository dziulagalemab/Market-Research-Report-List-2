<p><p>Glass Fiber Reinforced Plastic (GFRP) rebar, also known as Fiberglass Reinforced Polymer (FRP) rebar or Composite Rebar, is a reinforcement material used in concrete structures. It is made by combining glass fibers and a polymer resin matrix. GFRP rebar has gained popularity in recent years due to its superior properties such as high tensile strength, corrosion resistance, and lightweight nature compared to traditional steel rebar.</p><p>The market for GFRP rebar has been growing steadily. Manufacturers are investing in research and development to improve the mechanical properties of GFRP rebar, making it a suitable alternative to steel rebar. Additionally, the use of GFRP rebar in harsh environments, such as marine structures and coastal areas, is gaining traction due to its corrosion resistance properties.</p><p>Overall, the GFRP rebar market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years due to the increasing demand for durable and sustainable construction materials.
